# HomeScout Skilled Worker Visa Program Filter

Snapshot date: 2026-07-05.

This page filters HomeScout accelerator options through the lens of a UK Skilled Worker visa holder.
It is a product/startup planning note, not immigration advice. Before taking funding, becoming a
director, joining a full-time accelerator, changing employment, or spending material paid time on
HomeScout, get advice from an OISC-regulated immigration adviser or solicitor.

## Core Visa Constraint

Source: [GOV.UK Skilled Worker additional work](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/second-job)

GOV.UK says a Skilled Worker visa holder can work up to 20 hours a week in another job or for their
own business while still doing the sponsored job, as long as the extra work fits the listed
conditions. GOV.UK also says business administration, including preparing invoices, counts towards
the 20 hours.

Practical meaning for HomeScout:

- keep HomeScout work clearly outside sponsored-job hours
- keep a weekly time log
- avoid anything that looks like full-time founder work while still relying on Skilled Worker
  permission
- treat paid work, invoicing, director duties, accelerator obligations, customer delivery, pitch
  events, and funding administration as potentially counting towards the limit
- do not rely on an accelerator acceptance as proof that immigration conditions are satisfied

## Better Long-Term Visa Routes To Explore

### Innovator Founder Visa

Sources:

- [GOV.UK Innovator Founder visa](https://www.gov.uk/innovator-founder-visa)
- [GOV.UK endorsing bodies list](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/endorsing-bodies-innovator-founder-and-scale-up-visas)

Why it matters:

- This is the route designed for setting up and running an innovative UK business.
- GOV.UK says the business must be new, innovative, viable, and scalable.
- GOV.UK lists approved endorsing bodies; as of the 20 April 2026 update, the business endorsing
  bodies are UK Endorsing Services, Innovator International, and Envestors Limited. The Global
  Entrepreneurs Programme can issue Innovator Founder endorsements only for founders invited onto
  that programme.

HomeScout fit:

- HomeScout can make an Innovator Founder argument if it proves the market gap:
  evidence-backed renter and buyer decision packs, not generic property search.
- It will need a stronger proof pack than a normal accelerator application: user validation,
  competitive differentiation, commercial plan, scalability, and job-creation/growth logic.

Risk:

- Endorsement is not automatic.
- The application costs and contact-point costs are meaningful.
- The bar is not "nice app"; it is innovative, viable, and scalable.

### Global Talent Visa - Digital Technology

Sources:

- [GOV.UK Global Talent visa](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent)
- [Tech Nation Global Talent Visa](https://technation.io/global-talent-visa/)

Why it matters:

- This route is tied to the founder's personal technology profile, not only the startup.
- Tech Nation describes the route as flexible enough to change jobs or start up without updating
  immigration status.

HomeScout fit:

- Stronger if the founder can evidence technical leadership, product impact, open-source or public
  technical work, innovation, industry recognition, or meaningful startup traction.
- HomeScout can become part of the evidence, but the application is about the person as much as the
  company.

Risk:

- The evidence bar can be high.
- It may be better after HomeScout has public product proof, users, press, awards, revenue, or
  credible technical artifacts.

## High-Fit Program Types

### Best Fit While Still On Skilled Worker: Low-Commitment Support

These are useful because they can support HomeScout without immediately forcing a full-time founder
commitment.

#### Microsoft For Startups

Source: [Microsoft for Startups](https://startups.microsoft.com/)

Why it fits:

- HomeScout is already Microsoft-stack aligned: .NET, Aspire, Azure, and Microsoft Foundry Agent
  Service.
- Credits and technical resources can help build without needing a full-time accelerator schedule.

Visa-holder note:

- Good early option if participation is self-paced and does not require full-time founder work.
- Still track time spent on HomeScout.

Priority: high.

#### NatWest Accelerator

Source: [NatWest Accelerator](https://www.natwest.com/business/business-services/entrepreneur-accelerator.html)

Why it fits:

- NatWest positions it as a community and support platform with virtual access, hubs, tools,
  coaching, connections, masterclasses, and events.
- It is useful for learning sales, funding, leadership, and business basics while HomeScout is still
  being validated.

Visa-holder note:

- Better fit than a full-time residential accelerator because support can be used around work.
- Confirm eligibility and keep participation within the Skilled Worker additional-work rules.

Priority: high.

#### Founder Institute

Source: [Founder Institute](https://fi.co/join)

Why it fits:

- Founder Institute has London and global cohorts and is designed for early-stage founders.
- The page shows hybrid delivery and scheduled evening sessions for some cohorts.

Visa-holder note:

- Useful only if the workload, incorporation expectations, and any business activity remain
  visa-safe.
- It may push founders toward formal company-building earlier than is wise without immigration
  advice.

Priority: medium, with legal check first.

### Best Fit For HomeScout But Needs Visa Review

These are strong for the product, but less straightforward for a Skilled Worker holder because they
can involve funding, intensive participation, public pitching, or founder obligations.

#### Geovation

Source: [Geovation Accelerator](https://geovation.uk/accelerator/)

Why it fits:

- This is still the strongest HomeScout product fit.
- It supports location, land, and property data-driven startups.
- The programme offers up to GBP 20,000 equity-free funding, data access, expert support, workshops,
  showcase opportunities, and workspace.

Visa-holder note:

- High product fit, but funding and 6 months of intensive support need immigration review.
- Do not enter unless the time commitment, funding treatment, director role, and business activity
  are confirmed as compatible with current permission or a switch plan.

Priority: high after immigration advice.

#### REACH UK

Source: [REACH UK](https://www.nar-reach.com/uk)

Why it fits:

- Strong real-estate network fit for HomeScout after pilots.
- Useful for partner conversations with agents, brokers, and property-industry operators.

Visa-holder note:

- Treat as a later step once HomeScout has pilot proof and a visa plan.
- Ask explicitly whether international founders or Skilled Worker visa holders can participate.

Priority: high later.

#### Pi Labs

Source: [Pi Labs](https://pilabs.vc/)

Why it fits:

- Strong proptech and built-world investor fit.
- Better once HomeScout has traction and a credible venture story.

Visa-holder note:

- Investment discussions, founder obligations, and company setup need legal review.
- Better after deciding whether to switch to Innovator Founder or Global Talent.

Priority: medium-high later.

### Visa-Transition Programs And Endorsing Bodies

These are not accelerators in the normal sense, but they matter because they may support a legal
route from Skilled Worker to founder.

#### UK Endorsing Services

Sources:

- [GOV.UK endorsing bodies list](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/endorsing-bodies-innovator-founder-and-scale-up-visas)
- [UK Endorsing Services](https://www.ukesapp.co.uk/)

Why it fits:

- It is listed by GOV.UK as a business endorsing body.
- Its site says Innovator Founder applications are assessed on innovation, viability, and
  scalability.

HomeScout angle:

- Prepare a proper business plan, financial plan, validation evidence, competitor positioning, and
  scalability story before contacting.

Priority: high if switching to Innovator Founder becomes the plan.

#### Innovator International

Sources:

- [GOV.UK endorsing bodies list](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/endorsing-bodies-innovator-founder-and-scale-up-visas)
- [Innovator International](https://www.innovatorinternational.com/)

Why it fits:

- It is listed by GOV.UK as a business endorsing body.
- Its site describes a UK Growth Accelerator for international entrepreneurs and says successful
  endorsement applicants become part of its community.

HomeScout angle:

- Strong if HomeScout is framed as an innovation-rich UK company with clear expansion potential.

Priority: high if switching to Innovator Founder becomes the plan.

#### Envestors Limited

Source: [GOV.UK endorsing bodies list](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/endorsing-bodies-innovator-founder-and-scale-up-visas)

Why it fits:

- It is listed by GOV.UK as a business endorsing body.

HomeScout angle:

- Research current process directly before engaging; the public page was not easy to inspect in this
  snapshot.

Priority: medium-high if switching to Innovator Founder becomes the plan.

#### Global Entrepreneurs Programme

Source: [GOV.UK endorsing bodies list](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/endorsing-bodies-innovator-founder-and-scale-up-visas)

Why it fits:

- GOV.UK says the Global Entrepreneurs Programme is for internationally mobile, tech-based
  entrepreneurs and helps them scale innovation-rich companies from a UK global HQ.
- GOV.UK also says it only provides visa endorsements for founders already invited to participate in
  the programme.

HomeScout angle:

- Possible later if HomeScout becomes clearly innovation-rich and internationally scalable.

Priority: aspirational, not the first move.

## Lower-Fit Or Higher-Risk While On Skilled Worker

These can still be good programs, but they may be awkward for someone still bound to a sponsored job.

- Full-time founder accelerators that expect daily participation.
- Programs that require relocation for months.
- Programs that require immediate incorporation, director duties, or paid customer delivery before
  visa advice.
- Programs that give funding tied to active founder labour without checking immigration treatment.
- Programs that require leaving the sponsored employer before a new route is secured.

Examples to treat carefully: YC, Techstars, Antler, Entrepreneur First, Founders Factory, and any
intensive investor-led accelerator. They may be good later, but they should follow visa strategy,
not lead it.

## Recommended Path

1. Keep building HomeScout around the sponsored job, within the 20-hour/additional-work rules.
2. Use low-commitment support first: Microsoft For Startups, NatWest Accelerator, founder events,
   and mentor calls.
3. Run the HomeScout pilot proof pack: real listings, real users, anonymised decision packs, and
   willingness-to-pay evidence.
4. Speak to an immigration adviser before taking accelerator funding or becoming visibly active as a
   full-time founder.
5. Decide the route:
   - stay on Skilled Worker and keep HomeScout as a controlled side project
   - switch to Innovator Founder if HomeScout has a strong scalable-business case
   - explore Global Talent if the founder's personal tech evidence is strong enough
6. Apply to Geovation once the visa position is clear.

## Questions To Ask Every Accelerator

- Can a UK Skilled Worker visa holder participate?
- Is the programme part-time, full-time, or self-paced?
- Does the programme require incorporation, becoming a director, or issuing invoices?
- Is funding a grant, prize, stipend, investment, or payment for work?
- How many hours per week are expected?
- Are there mandatory weekday sessions during normal working hours?
- Do they provide immigration guidance, or should applicants use their own adviser?
- Do they accept founders who are employed full-time during the programme?
